What did we accomplish? Roadblocks? Feedback?

 

Following our plan from the previous sprint, we completed the about page, list of new events, and populate new events page with user inputs. These tasks turned out to be fairly straightforward though each team member encountered technical challenges along the way.

 

We began the week with a standup meeting where we divided projects in Waffle. We then met for a coding session so as to be able to help each other when we encountered roadblocks. The coding was completed by the following morning.

 

One challenge that we confronted was team members attempting to help others by working on their projects. In our retrospective, we further clarified that assigned stories would only be worked on by the person assigned. Also, in our retrospective, we noticed that points we listed in our previous report were average as opposed to Fibonacci. We have corrected those in both our burn-down chart and the product backlog below.

 

Overall, team members feel comfortable with the amount of work they are assigned and the workflow process developed to ensure version control.

What is planned for sprint 3?

 

In sprint two we plan to focus on story H (allowing users to create events). Since this is a sizeable project we will only plan to complete this one assignment. We will hold a group coding session to ensure that everyone is involved in the work. If it seems to be going well we may begin stories B and F, but we may also choose to wait for feedback and use the time to fix bugs or absorb new features from management.
